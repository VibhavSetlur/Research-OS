### theory_math

## Summary

The theory_math pack is **structurally registered but functionally only ~30% wired into v2.0.0's eight core subsystems**. The bones are right: it registers via `PackRegistration`, declares the correct non-IMRAD `paper_sections=("introduction","preliminaries","main_theorems","proofs","discussion")`, ships 8 protocols + 3 tools (lean_check / coq_check / dep_graph), and `_resolve_paper_sections` correctly looks up the schema at synthesis time. The two N-A cells (dashboard_story, dashboard_explore) are genuinely N-A — theory work has no tabular data, and the dep-graph IS the visual artifact. But every other "publishing surface" subsystem is still empirical-shaped: the literature gate's verdict vocabulary (AGREES/DISAGREES/EXTENDS) doesn't match how math papers cite prior theorems; the 13 typst venue templates have no theorem/proof environments; the 5 slide and 5 poster templates are all IMRAD-skinned; the 7 default personas have no proof-specific lens; and the drafter_loop's `expected_sections` plumbing exists but isn't passed by `server.py`, so even the section-coverage metric still measures generic h2 hits. None of these are deep architectural problems — most are 30-100 LOC additions (one venue template, one slide skeleton, one poster template, 2-3 persona YAMLs, ~5 LOC in each of three server handlers). The pack will be **complete enough to ship in v2.0.0** if those gaps get closed, but as of `feat/v2.0.0` HEAD a researcher running `tool_paper_compile_typst` on a Lean project gets a paper with the right section ORDER but no theorem typesetting, and a poster/slides that look like an experimental-biology deliverable with theorem statements jammed in.
